The Upwork Foundation

Envisioning a future of inclusive economic opportunity so people have better lives.

The Upwork Foundation is a philanthropic initiative created in connection with Upwork’s initial public offering. Funded in 2018 by a charitable donation of 500,000 shares of Upwork common stock, all contributions are made through a donor-advised fund at the Tides Foundation.

Foundation giving initiatives

In an effort to close the global opportunity gap, The Upwork Foundation funds initiatives that connect marginalized communities with life-sustaining knowledge work, no matter their location. We primarily fund organizations serving the following communities:

  • BIPOC
  • Women
  • Disadvantage youth and young aldults
  • People with disablities
  • Refugees, Immigrants and Asylum Seekers
  • LGBTQIA+
  • Veterans
  • Older professionals
  • Rural communities
  • Communities in developing countries

2025 Focus: Expanding Human Potential for Tech-Enabled Work

Advanced AI is transforming industries, redefining job roles, and revolutionizing the way work gets done. As automation takes on more tasks, we are challenged to identify the uniquely human skillsets that enable professional growth, economic mobility and deeper human connection at work.

The Upwork Foundation's 2025 grant-making strategy is dedicated to expanding human potential and strengthening the following human-centric skills that empower individuals in a tech-enabled workplace:

  • Creativity
  • Empathy
  • Integrity
  • Critical Thinking
  • Communication
  • Leadership

We aim to support nonprofits in workforce development that are designing and implementing programs around the essential skills listed above. Priority will be given to organizations that actively leverage technology to advance these skills.

  • Must be a registered 501(c)(3) charitable organization, have a US-based 501(c)(3) fiscal sponsor, or have obtained a valid equivalency determination.
  • Must primarily serve low-income communities. This can include communities in low- and lower-middle income countries as defined by the World Bank and/or individuals from households whose incomes fall below the state or national
  • Must focus primarily on workforce development and entrepreneurship, which may include job and skills-based trainings, job placement services, career advancement programs, or start-up accelerators.

Our application process is invitation-only. If your organization’s work is clearly tied to our 2025 giving focus and meets ALL of the requirements listed above, fill out this brief interest form no later than April 28. If we see alignment, we will contact you with further information.
